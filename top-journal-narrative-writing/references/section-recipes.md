# Section Recipes

## Title recipes

### Descriptive

[Intervention/mechanism/dataset] [verb] [specific outcome] in [system/context]

Example pattern: “Single-cell mapping identifies [process] in [system]”

### Mechanistic

[Mechanism/principle] enables/drives/limits [phenomenon] in [system]

### Significance-led

[Field-level advance] through/by [specific mechanism or evidence]

## Abstract recipe

1. Context: one sentence understandable outside the subfield.
2. Gap: one sentence naming the unresolved problem or tension.
3. Approach: one sentence on decisive design, data, model, or method.
4. Finding: one to two sentences with the main result, preferably specific.
5. Implication: one sentence on how the field changes; include boundary if needed.

### Abstract self-check

- Can a non-specialist scientist understand the first and last sentence?
- Does the abstract state the main result, not just the topic?
- Are quantitative or mechanistic details included where they add credibility?
- Are methods details limited to what is necessary?
- Are claims no stronger than the evidence?

## Introduction recipe

### Paragraph 1: field-level problem

Start broad but not generic. Avoid textbook background. State the problem and stakes.

### Paragraph 2: current knowledge and limitation

Summarize closest prior work. Focus on the limitation that your study resolves.

### Paragraph 3: tension/gap/barrier

Define the unresolved question. Explain why it has resisted solution.

### Paragraph 4: contribution and roadmap

Use a “Here we…” sentence. State the main finding and the evidence logic.

## Results recipe

For each results subsection:

1. Rationale: why this analysis/experiment is needed.
2. Design: what was done, briefly.
3. Observation: what was found.
4. Interpretation: what the result means for the main claim.
5. Transition: why the next result is needed.

## Discussion recipe

1. Main answer in one sentence.
2. Advance over prior work.
3. Mechanism/principle/model.
4. Broader implications.
5. Limitations and boundary conditions.
6. Specific next steps, if useful.

## Cover letter recipe

Dear Editors,

We submit “[title]” for consideration as a [article type]. The study reports [one-sentence central discovery].

This advance is important because [field-level problem] has been limited by [gap/barrier]. By [approach/evidence], we show [specific contribution], which [why this changes understanding or practice].

We believe the manuscript is appropriate for [journal] because [fit with broad readership/scope] and because the findings will interest researchers in [field 1], [field 2], and [field 3].

[Compliance notes: related manuscripts, conflicts, data/code/materials, suggested/excluded reviewers if requested.]

Sincerely,
[Corresponding author]
