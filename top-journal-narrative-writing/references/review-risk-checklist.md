# Review Risk Checklist

## Desk-rejection risks

- The manuscript lacks one central claim.
- The advance is framed only as “we applied method X to system Y.”
- The Introduction reads as background rather than tension.
- The abstract does not state a concrete result.
- Figures are ordered chronologically rather than logically.
- Importance is limited to a narrow subfield but the target journal needs broad readership.
- The cover letter repeats the abstract instead of explaining journal fit.
- Claims rely on unsupported novelty language: first, unprecedented, paradigm-shifting, revolutionary.
- Results are incremental relative to a close prior paper not discussed.

## Reviewer risks

- Key claim lacks direct evidence.
- Controls, benchmarks, null models, or sensitivity analyses are missing.
- Alternative explanations are not considered.
- Statistical details are unclear.
- Methods are insufficient for replication.
- Sample size, exclusion criteria, or data preprocessing are underreported.
- Results and Discussion overstate causality from correlation.
- Literature review ignores conflicting findings.
- Data/code/materials availability is vague.

## Language risks

Replace vague or inflated wording:

| Risky wording | Safer alternative |
|---|---|
| “proves” | “shows,” “supports,” or “is consistent with” |
| “novel” | specify what is new |
| “first” | use only with documented search and precise scope |
| “important implications” | name the implication |
| “significantly improves” | give metric and comparison |
| “robust” | state robustness test |
| “universal” | state tested conditions |
| “mechanism” | use only when mechanism is directly tested |

## Final pre-submission check

- Does every major claim point to a figure, table, or analysis?
- Does every figure contribute to the main claim?
- Can the abstract stand alone?
- Does the Introduction create a gap rather than a history lesson?
- Does the Discussion acknowledge boundaries without weakening the contribution?
- Are target-journal word limits, display-item limits, and AI-use policies verified?
